# Risk and Return in Investing

One of the most fundamental concepts in investing is the relationship between risk and return: generally, higher potential returns come with higher risk. Understanding this trade-off helps you make informed investment decisions aligned with your goals and temperament.

## Types of Investment Risk

**Market Risk**: The overall market declining affects most investments. Also called systematic risk, it cannot be diversified away.

**Company Risk**: A specific company performs poorly. This can be reduced through diversification.

**Inflation Risk**: Returns don't keep pace with inflation, eroding purchasing power.

**Liquidity Risk**: Unable to sell an investment quickly at fair value.

**Concentration Risk**: Too much of your portfolio in one stock, sector, or asset class.

## Risk Tolerance

Your risk tolerance depends on:
- **Time horizon**: Longer time horizons can absorb more volatility
- **Financial situation**: Emergency fund, income stability, existing savings
- **Emotional comfort**: Will you panic-sell during a market crash?

## Risk Profiles

**Conservative**: Prioritizes capital preservation. Portfolio: ~30% stocks, 70% bonds/cash. Expected return: 4-5% annually. Suitable for: near-retirement, short time horizons.

**Moderate**: Balanced approach. Portfolio: ~60% stocks, 40% bonds. Expected return: 6-7% annually. Suitable for: most investors with 10+ year horizons.

**Aggressive**: Maximum growth. Portfolio: ~90% stocks, 10% bonds. Expected return: 8-10% annually. Suitable for: young investors, high risk tolerance.

## Volatility vs. Risk

Volatility (short-term price swings) is not the same as permanent loss. A stock that drops 30% in a year but recovers over 5 years is volatile, not necessarily a bad investment. True risk is permanent loss of capital.

## The Risk-Return Spectrum

From lowest to highest risk (and expected return):
1. Money market / savings accounts
2. Government bonds
3. Investment-grade corporate bonds
4. High-yield bonds
5. Large-cap stocks
6. Small-cap stocks
7. Emerging market stocks
8. Individual stocks / options / crypto
