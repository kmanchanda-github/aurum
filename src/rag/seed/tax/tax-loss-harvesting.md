# Tax-Loss Harvesting: Turning Losses into Tax Savings

Tax-loss harvesting is the practice of selling investments at a loss to offset capital gains taxes on investments sold at a profit. It turns portfolio losses — an inevitable part of investing — into real tax savings.

## The Basic Mechanics

Suppose you have:
- $5,000 gain from selling Apple stock
- $2,000 loss in an energy ETF you're holding

By selling the energy ETF and realizing the $2,000 loss, you reduce your taxable gain to $3,000. If you're in the 15% capital gains bracket, this saves $300 in taxes (15% × $2,000).

## Short-Term vs. Long-Term Losses

Losses first offset gains of the same type:
- Short-term losses offset short-term gains first (taxed as ordinary income — higher rate)
- Long-term losses offset long-term gains first (taxed at 0%, 15%, or 20%)

If losses exceed gains, you can deduct up to $3,000 against ordinary income annually. Excess losses carry forward to future years indefinitely.

## The Wash-Sale Rule

The IRS doesn't let you sell a security at a loss and immediately buy it back. The **wash-sale rule** prohibits repurchasing a "substantially identical" security within 30 days before or after the sale.

Workarounds:
- Wait 31 days before repurchasing
- Buy a similar-but-not-identical ETF (sell a Vanguard S&P 500 ETF, buy an iShares S&P 500 ETF — they track the same index but are not "substantially identical")
- Invest in a different sector fund temporarily

## When Tax-Loss Harvesting Makes Sense

Tax-loss harvesting is most valuable when:
- You have significant realized gains in the same year
- You're in a higher tax bracket
- The tax savings outweigh any tracking error from switching funds

It's less valuable in tax-advantaged accounts (IRAs, 401ks) where there are no capital gains taxes to offset.
