# Building an Emergency Fund

An emergency fund is money set aside specifically for unexpected expenses or income disruption. It's the financial foundation that allows you to invest confidently without risking your long-term investments.

## Why You Need One First

Without an emergency fund, you may be forced to:
- Sell investments at a loss during a market downturn
- Take on high-interest debt for unexpected expenses
- Make stress-driven financial decisions

## How Much to Save

**General guideline**: 3-6 months of essential expenses.

Calculate your monthly essentials:
- Rent/mortgage
- Utilities and insurance
- Groceries
- Minimum debt payments
- Transportation

**Example**: If your monthly essentials are $3,000, your target emergency fund is $9,000-$18,000.

Adjust based on your situation:
- Stable job + dual income: 3 months is sufficient
- Variable income (freelancer, commission): 6-12 months
- Single income household: 6 months
- Industry with layoff risk: 6+ months

## Where to Keep It

Emergency funds should be:
1. **Liquid**: Accessible within 1-2 days
2. **Safe**: Not subject to market risk
3. **Separate**: Psychologically separate from checking to avoid spending it

**Best options**:
- High-Yield Savings Account (HYSA): Currently 4-5% APY, FDIC insured
- Money Market Account: Similar yields, also FDIC insured
- Short-term Treasury bills (T-bills): Slightly higher yield, accessible via TreasuryDirect

**Avoid**: CDs (early withdrawal penalties), investments (market risk), checking accounts (too accessible and lower interest).

## Building the Fund

If starting from scratch:
1. Open a dedicated HYSA immediately
2. Set up automatic transfers each payday (even $50/week = $2,600/year)
3. Direct windfalls (tax refunds, bonuses) to the fund
4. Pause other investing until you reach your target

Once funded, only use it for genuine emergencies and replenish ASAP after using it.
