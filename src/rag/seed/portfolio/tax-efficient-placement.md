# Tax-Efficient Asset Placement

If you have both taxable brokerage accounts and tax-advantaged accounts (IRA, 401k, Roth IRA), where you hold each investment matters almost as much as what you hold. Asset location — placing the right investments in the right account type — can add 0.5–1.5% per year to your after-tax returns without changing your portfolio at all.

## The Core Principle

Different investments generate different types of taxable events. Match high-tax investments with tax-sheltered accounts; let low-tax investments live in taxable accounts.

## Account Types and Their Tax Treatment

| Account | Contributions | Growth | Withdrawals |
|---|---|---|---|
| 401(k) / Traditional IRA | Pre-tax | Tax-deferred | Taxed as income |
| Roth IRA / Roth 401(k) | After-tax | Tax-free | Tax-free |
| Taxable brokerage | After-tax | Taxed annually | Capital gains tax |

## What Goes Where

### Best in Tax-Deferred Accounts (Traditional IRA, 401k)
These generate ordinary income when withdrawn — so hold assets that would otherwise generate ordinary income annually:

- **Bonds and bond funds**: Interest is taxed at ordinary income rates (up to 37%). Sheltering this in a 401k is highly valuable.
- **REITs**: Dividends are typically ordinary income, not qualified dividends.
- **High-turnover actively managed funds**: Frequent trading generates short-term capital gains taxed at ordinary rates.
- **Inflation-protected bonds (TIPS)**: Imputed inflation adjustments are taxed annually even without selling.

### Best in Roth IRA
Roth accounts grow tax-free forever — ideal for assets with the highest expected long-term growth:

- **Small-cap and growth stocks**: Expected to grow the most; sheltering in Roth means no taxes on larger gains
- **High-growth individual stocks** with concentrated upside
- **Aggressive stock ETFs** for young investors with decades of compounding ahead

### Best in Taxable Accounts
These generate minimal annual taxes and are most compatible with taxable brokerage accounts:

- **Broad stock index funds** (VTI, VOO): Low turnover, mostly qualified dividends taxed at 0–20%
- **Municipal bonds**: Interest is federally tax-exempt (best for high earners)
- **Tax-managed funds**: Engineered to minimize capital gains distributions
- **I-Bonds and EE Bonds**: Interest can be deferred until redemption

## Practical Example

Suppose you have:
- $60,000 in a Roth IRA
- $40,000 in a Traditional 401k
- $50,000 in a taxable brokerage

**Suboptimal placement**: All accounts hold a 60/40 stock/bond mix.

**Optimized placement**:
- Roth IRA: 100% stocks (small-cap growth ETF)
- 401k: 100% bonds (intermediate bond fund)
- Taxable: Broad stock index funds (VTI/VXUS)

Same overall allocation, but far less tax drag each year.

## Rebalancing Complexity

With multiple accounts, rebalancing becomes more complex — you must rebalance across accounts while maintaining location. Most investors review this annually or when an account grows significantly out of proportion.

## Bottom Line

Asset location is a free performance boost. Before changing what you own, consider whether you've optimized where you own it.
